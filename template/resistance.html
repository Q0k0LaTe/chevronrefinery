Resistance is Fertile

Richmond is not just a victim; it is a leader in the Environmental Justice movement.


Asian Pacific Environmental Network (APEN)

Organizing the Laotian refugee community in Richmond to fight for better evacuation warnings and stricter emission caps.


Richmond Progressive Alliance (RPA)

A grassroots political party that has successfully elected corporate-free candidates to the City Council, challenging Chevron's political spending.

Communities for a Better Environment (CBE)

Combines legal challenges with youth organizing; fought Chevron’s permits and championed community air monitoring.


Important Moments

  • 2012 Fire: 15k residents sought medical care, catalyzing mass organizing.
  • Rule 6-5: Community pressure forced BAAQMD to cap refinery particulates.
  • Ballot-box wins: RPA candidates reshaped city council majorities.

"We are not a sacrifice zone."

Richmond organizers link environmental health to housing justice and immigrant rights—showing how EJ fights are as much about democracy as they are about emissions.

Redlining & Segregation

Mapping Inequality’s 1937 survey steered Black, Asian, and Latino families toward the very land zoned for heavy industry—laying the groundwork for today’s refinery fence-line neighborhoods.

HOLC Map

Hazardous by Design

Federal Home Owners' Loan Corporation (HOLC) graders painted north and west Richmond in red—“hazardous”—explicitly citing “proximity to Standard Oil.” Those ratings suppressed investment while legitimizing toxic land uses.

  • A–B zones: Farther from industry; reserved for white homeowners.
  • C–D zones: Adjacent to refineries and rail; open to Black and Asian families.

Segregated Geographies

How Segregation Happened

Restrictive covenants, racially filtered realtors, and wartime public housing funneled Black shipyard workers and Asian refugees into North Richmond. Zoning kept the densest housing closest to rail spurs and refinery stacks.

Why It Matters Today

Current asthma and cancer risks still follow these boundaries—proving that historical maps still predict modern health outcomes.

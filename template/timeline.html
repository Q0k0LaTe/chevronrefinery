Richmond + Chevron Timeline

A century of industry, migration, and environmental justice activism condensed into key moments.

1900–1940

Industrial Entry

Standard Oil opens the refinery (1902) and builds rail and tank infrastructure along the shoreline.

1940–1970

WWII & Migration

Shipyard jobs bring Black southerners and Asian refugees; wartime housing clusters near the refinery.

1970–2000

Environmental Justice Emerges

Federal Clean Air Act rules meet local organizing; early lawsuits challenge refinery emissions and accidents.

2000–2020

Political Power Shift

Chevron bankrolls city elections while the Richmond Progressive Alliance wins council seats; 2012 fire triggers medical surge and settlements.

2020–Present

Climate Futures

Sea-level rise threatens toxic sites; AB 617 monitoring expands; community push for just transition grows.

Wartime shipbuilding tied Richmond’s housing and labor force to refinery growth.

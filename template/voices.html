Community Voices

Environmental racism is lived experience. These snapshots highlight how Richmond residents describe life near the refinery—and how they organize for cleaner air.

Resident Mini-Profiles

  • Iron Triangle parent: “We keep inhalers in every backpack because the flares make my kids wheeze.”
  • North Richmond elder: “We were steered here during the war; the refinery has never left us.”
  • Youth organizer: “EJ is about housing, jobs, and air—one fight.”

These testimonies draw from APEN’s community listening sessions and local reporting after major flare events.

Quotes From the Fence Line

“During the 2012 fire, we didn’t know where to go. Sirens blared but no one told us if the air was safe.”

“Chevron’s donations don’t clean the air—we need monitoring and real caps on emissions.”

Why Voices Matter

Environmental justice insists that impacted residents define the problem and the solutions. Their testimonies pressure regulators, shape public health protocols, and keep corporations accountable.

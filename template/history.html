Chevron Refinery History

From its 1902 origins as Standard Oil to the 2012 fire, the Richmond refinery has expanded, flared, and reshaped the shoreline—and the lives of neighbors downwind.

Industrial Roots

Built in 1902 to refine crude arriving by tanker and rail, the plant grew during World Wars I and II. Housing for workers was sited nearby—foreshadowing the fence-line neighborhoods of today.

  • 1902: Standard Oil opens the Richmond refinery.
  • 1940s: WWII shipyard boom demands more fuel; capacity expands.
  • 1980s–2000s: Regulatory fights over emissions and safety upgrades.
1902

Standard Oil Arrives

Refinery begins operating along the Richmond shoreline, anchoring heavy industry next to working-class housing.

1940s

Wartime Expansion

WWII shipbuilding brings rapid population growth; Richmond adds wartime housing close to refinery stacks and rail spurs.

1980s–2000s

Regulation & Violations

Community lawsuits and Bay Area Air Quality Management District (BAAQMD) fines challenge repeated flaring and safety lapses.

2012

Vapor Cloud Fire

A corroded pipe ruptures, sending 15,000 residents for medical care and spurring a surge in environmental justice organizing.

Key Pollutants

  • PM2.5 & NOx: Everyday combustion and flaring.
  • Benzene: Carcinogenic emissions from leaks and flares.
  • SOx & heavy metals: Threaten respiratory and cardiovascular health.

Violations & Fines

EPA settlements and BAAQMD enforcement actions repeatedly cite Chevron for flare events, toxic releases, and delayed notifications to residents.
